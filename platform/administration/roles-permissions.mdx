Microset uses role-based access control (RBAC) to let you define precisely what each person on your team can see and do. Roles are assigned at the workspace level when a member is invited, but you can override them on any individual project to give someone more or less access without changing their workspace-wide permissions.

## Built-in Roles

Every Microset workspace comes with three built-in roles that cover the most common access patterns. You cannot edit or delete these roles, but you can supplement them with custom roles when you need finer control.

<CardGroup cols={3}>
  <Card title="Admin" icon="shield">
    Full access to workspace settings, billing, all projects, and member management. Admins can create and delete projects, invite or deactivate members, configure security policies, and manage integrations. There must always be at least one Admin in a workspace.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Member" icon="user">
    Can create projects, manage content within those projects, and invite collaborators to projects they own or administer. Members cannot access workspace-level settings, billing, or the Users management panel.
  </Card>
  <Card title="Viewer" icon="eye">
    Read-only access to projects they are explicitly added to. Viewers can browse content and leave comments but cannot create, edit, or delete any items. They have no access to projects they have not been individually invited to.
  </Card>
</CardGroup>

## Custom Roles

Custom roles let you define a precise permission set that does not map cleanly to the built-in options. You choose exactly which capabilities to enable, then assign the role to any member in your workspace.

<Info>
  Custom roles are available on the **Business** and **Enterprise** plans. Each workspace supports up to **20** custom roles. To upgrade your plan, visit **Settings → Billing**.
</Info>

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open the Roles panel">
    Navigate to **Settings → Roles** and click **New role**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Name your role">
    Enter a clear, descriptive name and an optional description. Both are visible to admins when assigning the role to members.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Configure permissions">
    Toggle each permission on or off. Permissions are grouped by area — Projects, Analytics, Integrations, Billing, and Settings — so you can quickly scope a role to a specific function.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Save and assign">
    Click **Save role**. The role is now available in the role selector when inviting members, importing via CSV, or editing an existing member's access from **Settings → Users**.
  </Step>
</Steps>

### Common custom role patterns

These examples illustrate how to configure permissions for three frequently requested role types. Use them as a starting point and adjust toggles to fit your organization's policies.

| Custom role | Permissions enabled | Permissions disabled |
|---|---|---|
| **Data analyst** | Read analytics dashboards, export reports | All settings, project editing, integrations management |
| **Integration manager** | Full integrations access (create, edit, delete), read project content | Project editing, workspace settings, billing, member management |
| **Billing contact** | View and edit billing settings, download invoices | All project access, workspace settings, member management, integrations |

<Tip>
  When creating custom roles, start with the minimum permissions required and add more only as needed. This principle of least privilege reduces the risk of accidental changes to sensitive areas of your workspace.
</Tip>

## Per-Project Role Overrides

Workspace roles define a member's default access level, but individual projects sometimes need a different permission set. A Viewer at the workspace level, for example, might need Member-level access in one specific project.

<Steps>
  <Step title="Open project settings">
    Navigate to the project where you want to adjust a member's role and click **Settings → Members**.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Find the member">
    Locate the member in the project members list. Their current effective role (workspace default or an existing override) is shown in the **Role** column.
  </Step>
  <Step title="Assign the project-level role">
    Click the role dropdown on that member's row and select the role you want them to have within this project. The change takes effect immediately.
  </Step>
</Steps>

<Note>
  A project-level role override is stored on the project, not on the member's profile. If you remove the override — by selecting **Use workspace default** from the role dropdown — the member's access automatically reverts to their workspace-level role. Deleting the project also removes all overrides associated with it.
</Note>

<Warning>
  Project-level overrides can grant a Member-scoped user Admin-level access within a single project, including the ability to manage that project's settings and members. Review project membership carefully when working with sensitive projects.
</Warning>

Your workspace settings give you central control over how Microset behaves for every member on your team. Navigate to **Settings → General** to access the configuration options described on this page. Changes you make here take effect immediately unless otherwise noted.

## General

The General section controls the core identity and locale settings for your workspace. Use the table below as a reference for each available field.

| Setting | Description |
|---|---|
| **Workspace name** | The display name shown throughout the UI, in email notifications, and on shared links. Update this any time without affecting integrations. |
| **Workspace slug** | A URL-safe identifier used in API calls and browser URLs (for example, `app.microset.io/your-slug`). Changing it updates all existing URLs — warn your team before making this change so they can update any saved bookmarks or integrations. |
| **Timezone** | The default timezone for date displays and all scheduled jobs in your workspace. Individual members can override this in their personal preferences without affecting the workspace default. |
| **Language** | The default display language applied to new members when they first join. Existing members keep whatever language they have set in their own preferences. |

<Tip>
If your team spans multiple timezones, set the workspace timezone to match your primary operating region, and encourage members in other zones to set their own preference under **Profile → Preferences**.
</Tip>

## Security

The Security section lets you define authentication policies that apply across your entire workspace. Use the tabs below to configure each area.

<Tabs>
  <Tab title="Session Settings">
    Session settings control how long a member stays authenticated before Microset requires them to sign in again.

    - **Session timeout** — Defaults to **7 days** of inactivity. You can shorten this to 1 hour, 24 hours, or 48 hours to match your organization's security policy.
    - **Force re-authentication** — When enabled, all active sessions are immediately invalidated and every member must log in again. Use this after a security incident or a credential rotation.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open Session Settings">
        Navigate to **Settings → Security → Session Settings**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Set the timeout duration">
        Choose your preferred timeout from the **Session timeout** dropdown.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Save your changes">
        Click **Save**. New sessions created after this point will use the updated timeout value.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Note>
      Changing the session timeout only affects sessions created after you save. Existing active sessions will continue until they reach the previous timeout value or the member logs out manually.
    </Note>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="IP Allowlist">
    The IP allowlist restricts workspace access to traffic originating from specific IP addresses or CIDR ranges. This is useful for organizations that route all traffic through a corporate VPN or a fixed office IP.

    <Warning>
      If you add an IP allowlist and your own current IP address is not included, you will lock yourself out of the workspace immediately. Always include your current IP — or your VPN's egress IP — before saving.
    </Warning>

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open the IP Allowlist panel">
        Navigate to **Settings → Security → IP Allowlist**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Add your allowed ranges">
        Enter one IP address or CIDR block per line (for example, `203.0.113.0/24`). You can add as many ranges as needed.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Verify your own IP is included">
        Confirm that the IP address you are currently using appears in the list before proceeding.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enable and save">
        Toggle **Enforce IP allowlist** to on, then click **Save**. Requests from unlisted IPs will receive a 403 response.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Tip>
      To find your current public IP address, visit [whatismyip.com](https://www.whatismyip.com) or run `curl ifconfig.me` in your terminal.
    </Tip>
  </Tab>
  <Tab title="SSO">
    Single Sign-On (SSO) lets your members authenticate through your existing identity provider instead of managing separate Microset passwords. Microset supports both **SAML 2.0** and **OIDC** providers.

    <Steps>
      <Step title="Open SSO configuration">
        Navigate to **Settings → Security → Single Sign-On**.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Choose your protocol">
        Select either **SAML 2.0** or **OIDC** depending on what your identity provider supports. Common providers and their recommended protocol are listed in the configuration panel.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enter your provider details">
        For **SAML 2.0**, provide your Identity Provider Metadata URL or paste the raw XML metadata. For **OIDC**, provide the Issuer URL, Client ID, and Client Secret from your provider's application settings.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Test the connection">
        Click **Test SSO connection** to verify that Microset can reach your identity provider and exchange tokens correctly before enforcing SSO.
      </Step>
      <Step title="Enforce SSO">
        Toggle **Enforce SSO** to on and click **Save**. Once enforced, password-based login is disabled for all non-admin members. Admins retain password access as a fallback.
      </Step>
    </Steps>

    <Warning>
      Once you enforce SSO, non-admin members can no longer log in with a password. Make sure every member has a valid account in your identity provider before enabling enforcement.
    </Warning>

    <Info>
      SSO enforcement does not apply to workspace admins, who can always authenticate with their Microset password. This prevents a misconfigured identity provider from locking all administrators out of the workspace.
    </Info>
  </Tab>
</Tabs>

## Notification Defaults

Notification defaults set the out-of-the-box notification behavior for new members who join your workspace. Existing members are not affected — they retain whatever notification preferences they have already configured.

| Notification type | Default behavior | Notes |
|---|---|---|
| **Email digest** | Weekly summary | Change to daily or disable entirely from this panel. New members receive the digest frequency set here. |
| **@-mention alerts** | Immediate | Sent as soon as a member is mentioned. This setting cannot be changed at the workspace level; members control it individually. |
| **Project updates** | Enabled | Covers status changes, new assignments, and completion events within projects the member belongs to. |

<Note>
  Notification defaults only apply at the moment a new member accepts their invitation. If you update these defaults later, existing members are not retroactively affected. Ask members to review their personal notification preferences under **Profile → Notifications** if you need consistent settings across the team.
</Note>

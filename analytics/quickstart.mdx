Microset Analytics gives you a direct path from raw data to shareable dashboards without writing a single line of SQL (unless you want to). This guide walks you through connecting a data source, creating a dashboard, adding a chart, and sharing it with your team.

## Before you begin

Make sure you have the following ready before you start:

- **Data source credentials** — hostname, username, password, or API key for the source you plan to connect
- **Admin or Member role** in your Microset workspace (Viewer-only accounts cannot add data sources)

<Note>
  If your data source sits behind a firewall, allowlist the Microset outbound IP addresses before testing a connection. You can find the full list under **Workspace Settings → Network → Outbound IPs**.
</Note>

## Steps

<Steps>
  <Step title="Connect a data source">
    Open **Analytics** in the left sidebar, then go to **Data sources → Add source**.

    Select the source type that matches your database, warehouse, or SaaS tool. Enter your credentials — hostname, port, database name, username, and password (or API key for SaaS sources). Click **Test connection** to validate the credentials before saving.

    Once the test passes, click **Save source**. Your new source appears in the Data sources list and is immediately available for charts.

    For a full walkthrough of every source type and all connection options, see [Connect Data](/analytics/connect-data).
  </Step>

  <Step title="Create a dashboard">
    Go to **Analytics → Dashboards → New dashboard**.

    Give your dashboard a meaningful name — something your team will recognise at a glance, such as *Sales Pipeline – Q3* or *API Health Overview*. Choose a folder if your workspace uses them to organise dashboards, then click **Create**.

    You now have a blank canvas ready to receive charts.
  </Step>

  <Step title="Add a chart">
    Inside your new dashboard, click **Add chart** in the top-right corner.

    1. **Select a data source** — choose the source you connected in Step 1.
    2. **Write or build a query** — use the visual query builder or switch to SQL mode to filter, aggregate, and join your data.
    3. **Choose a chart type** — pick from **Bar**, **Line**, **Area**, **Pie**, or **Number card** depending on what best represents your metric.
    4. **Configure axes** — for bar and line charts, map your x-axis to a time or category column and your y-axis to a numeric measure. Number cards show a single aggregated value.
    5. Click **Save chart** to add it to the dashboard.

    <Tip>
      Use a **Number card** for top-level KPIs — a single bold figure draws attention to the metrics that matter most before viewers scroll to the detailed charts.
    </Tip>
  </Step>

  <Step title="Share your dashboard">
    Click the **Share** button in the dashboard header.

    | Option | What it does |
    |---|---|
    | **Copy link** | Generates a direct URL. Respects workspace access controls. |
    | **Embed code** | Produces an `<iframe>` snippet you can paste into any web page or internal tool. |
    | **Access level** | Set to *Workspace* (anyone in your org), *Link* (anyone with the URL), or *Private* (only you). |

    Recipients with workspace access see live data every time they open the link. Embedded dashboards refresh on the schedule configured for each chart's data source.

    <Warning>
      Setting access level to **Link** makes the dashboard readable by anyone who has the URL, including people outside your workspace. Only use this for public-facing data.
    </Warning>
  </Step>
</Steps>
